Stat Weights & Priorities
1
Spell Power & Shadow Damage
Your primary damage stat. Every point directly increases the output of all your spells.
2
Hit Rating (16% cap)
The hard cap is 16%. Talents provide 10%, meaning you only need 6% from gear. Note: 1% spell resist from bosses can never be removed, but this is factored into the 16% cap.
3
Haste
Strong stat, but there are no passive haste stats on any items until Phase 3. Quagmirran's Eye has a proc that gives us haste & is our P1 BiS trinket until Dark Iron Smoking Pipe in Phase 2.
4
Critical Strike
Poor stat. You need 6 crit rating to equal the value of 1 spell power. An item with 50 spell power beats one with 25 spell power and 100 crit rating.

Always sim your character to confirm priorities for your specific gear setup.

Talents

Unlike MoP's simplified talent system, TBC has three full talent trees: Discipline, Holy, and Shadow. As a DPS Shadow Priest you invest heavily in Shadow, with leftover points going into Discipline for utility and mana sustainability. The builds below are your starting points.

Your go-to raiding build. Heavy Shadow investment for maximum sustained DPS, with Discipline providing Meditation for mana regen and a handful of utility talents.

A variant that picks up Shadow Nova, trading a minor DPS talent for a strong AoE interrupt. Useful in encounters where a quick interrupt or AoE burst is needed.

Built around survivability and crowd control. Takes Silence, Improved Vampiric Embrace, and Dispersion-adjacent tools. Less raw DPS but much harder to shut down.

What is the Analyser?

The Shadow Priest Analyser is a companion tool designed to help you dig through your raid logs and identify areas for improvement. Paste in your Warcraft Logs URL or ID & select your character and fight. Get actionable feedback on your rotation, Mind Flay delay, and more.
